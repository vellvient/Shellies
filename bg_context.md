# Background Context

## 1. The Core Problem: AI's "Invisible" Environmental Cost
During our initial brainstorming, our team identified a major issue: while AI use is rapidly expanding in education, there is a massive lack of public awareness regarding its environmental impact. Generative AI queries consume up to ten times the energy of a standard web search and require massive water-cooling systems for hyperscale data centers. However, because this digital waste happens in "the cloud," it is completely invisible to the average student, leading to emotional detachment.

## 2. The "Value-Action Gap" (VAG)
Our research is grounded in a psychological concept called the Value-Action Gap (VAG). This is the disconnect where people might possess pro-environmental attitudes (e.g., caring about climate change), but they fail to act sustainably in their digital habits because the damage isn't tangible like a physical plastic bottle. Our team wants to study this gap among Malaysian secondary and pre-university students.

## 3. The Inspiration: The Glasgow Study
Our methodology was heavily inspired by a recent study from the University of Glasgow. That study successfully changed behaviors by using digital posters to translate the abstract water cost of ChatGPT into human-scale, relatable metrics (like showing that a single ChatGPT email uses 500 mL of water).

## 4. The Solution: "Making the Invisible Visible"
To solve the lack of awareness and test our hypothesis, our team decided that a standard survey wasn't enough. Instead, we proposed using "vibe coding" to build an interactive web app that functions as an educational intervention. The app is designed to:
- Capture baseline awareness (Pre-survey).
- Calculate a student's personal carbon/water footprint using precise mathematical logic and Malaysian Grid Emission Factors.
- "Make the invisible visible" by translating the data into relatable physical analogies (like bathtubs of water or miles driven in a car).
- Measure if seeing this visual data successfully bridges the Value-Action Gap and changes their future behavioral intentions (Post-survey).

Ultimately, we are building this MVP to elevate our symposium submission from a standard theoretical paper into a fully realized, data-driven prototype that aligns with UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 4, 9, 12, and 13).
